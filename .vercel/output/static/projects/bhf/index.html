Designing the Future of Britannica Home Fashions

I worked as the design lead on a major iteration of Britannica’s home fashions internal software application. We took the platform in a bold new direction, focusing on standardized components, promoting consistency, and facilitating collaboration.

Chief Architect FullStack Developer Database Admin UI/UX Designer Project Manager Prototyper
Meet the Client

Britannica Home Fashions (BHF) is aworldwide frontrunner in home goods, renowned for its diverse product lines, grappled with the intricacies of supply chain and order management data.

The company’sglobal positionis reinforced by theirlongstanding reputation and robust collaborations with leading brands such as UGG, Pendleton, and Walmart; and is supported by a network of international offices and a comprehensive omnichannel distribution system.

The Challenge

Despite its global success, BHF acknowledged the need to optimize operations and enhance efficiency. Occasional communication gaps between departments hindered access to crucial information, potentially impacting production schedules, revenue, and reputation. The existing manual processes, although functional, presented opportunities for improvement to minimize errors and boost productivity.

Leadership recognized that a more integrated internal system would bridge communication gaps, streamline inventory management, and simplify financial reporting. The current system required upgrades to enhance performance and user experience. Investing in a robust, unified system would position BHF for continued growth and success.

Scalability Issues
Communication Gaps

Data Inaccuracy

To tackle this challenge, BHF hired me to develop a comprehensive ERP system from the ground up. The objective was to create a seamless platform that would improve processes, increase data accuracy, and deliver real-time insights. I constructed the system module by module, encompassing item creation, purchase orders, invoicing, vendor portal, DocuSign integration, API connections, and more. By unifying these components, BHF would operate more efficiently, reduce errors, and make informed, data-driven decisions.

As the system grew in complexity and scope, it became necessary to expand the development team to ensure effective maintenance and continuous improvement. I took on the role of a manager and mentor, guiding junior and intermediate developers as they joined the team. This transition allowed me to oversee the system's growth, delegate tasks, and provide guidance to the growing development team.
